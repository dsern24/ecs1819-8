Irene C. Hernandez

Influencers

George Dunne

Irene worked with Dunne, both of them impacting one another. After her death, he stated "She was a wonderful woman and a dedicated public servant who will be greatly missed. She was dedicated to the people she represented and to all the residents of Cook County."

Richard J. Daley

Irene was influenced by Mayor Richard Daley because she was appointed at his request to fill the empty spot on the Cook County Board. Without him, Irene would've been less likely to hold a position on the board because she was a Latina.

Richard Phelan

Phelan often turned to Irene for help and guidance saying she was "like an older sister." He stated "She was so caring and concerned about the public, yet she was very pragmatic and understood what was necessary to get the job done."

Irene C. Hernandez: First Latina on the Cook County Commissioners Board

Irene C. Hernandez was close to Mayor Richard Daley, and was very involved in Mexican-American problems and events. She volunteered in the woman's auxiliary and she helped to organize the first Mexican-American cotillion. Before being on the board, Hernandez worked as a stenographer in several export firms and served as a translator for cases. Throughout her life, Irene was concerned with helping those who were underpriveleged and tried to look out for their interests in political decisons. She later won elections in 1978, 1982, 1986, and 1990.
